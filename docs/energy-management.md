# Energy Management

## Purpose

The Energy Management layer coordinates electrical energy consumption, thermal energy recovery, and cooling operations across the Data Center Water & Heat Loop Ecosystem.

Its objective is to improve operational efficiency while supporting reliable cooling for AI and high-performance computing infrastructure.

---

# Primary Objectives

- Improve energy efficiency
- Reduce unnecessary power consumption
- Maximize heat recovery
- Improve cooling performance
- Support sustainable infrastructure

---

# Energy Sources

The system may operate with:

- Utility electrical power
- On-site renewable generation
- Battery energy storage
- Thermal energy storage
- Emergency backup power

---

# Energy Consumers

Major energy consumers include:

- AI compute clusters
- GPU servers
- CPU servers
- Cooling pumps
- Heat exchangers
- Cooling distribution units
- Water treatment equipment
- Monitoring infrastructure

---

# Thermal Energy Recovery

Recovered thermal energy may support:

- Building heating
- District heating
- Industrial process heating
- Greenhouses
- Aquaculture
- Water treatment facilities

---

# AI Optimization

Software may assist with:

- Pump scheduling
- Cooling optimization
- Heat routing
- Load balancing
- Capacity planning
- Demand forecasting

---

# Monitoring

Operational metrics may include:

- Total power consumption
- Pump energy
- Cooling energy
- Heat recovery output
- Thermal storage status
- System efficiency

---

# Long-Term Vision

Future versions may coordinate energy, cooling, and water systems through integrated monitoring and decision-support tools to improve overall operational performance.
