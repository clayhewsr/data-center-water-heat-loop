# Deployment

## Purpose

This document outlines a conceptual deployment strategy for implementing the Data Center Water & Heat Loop Ecosystem in new or existing AI and high-performance computing facilities.

---

# Phase 1 – Planning

Objectives:

- Define cooling requirements
- Estimate water demand
- Estimate heat generation
- Evaluate available water sources
- Identify heat reuse opportunities
- Develop preliminary system layout

Deliverables:

- Site assessment
- Initial system architecture
- Water balance estimate
- Heat recovery assessment

---

# Phase 2 – Infrastructure

Major components may include:

- Water treatment equipment
- Storage tanks
- Pump stations
- Cooling distribution piping
- Heat exchangers
- Thermal storage
- Monitoring equipment

---

# Phase 3 – Compute Integration

Connect cooling infrastructure to:

- AI servers
- GPU clusters
- CPU clusters
- Networking equipment
- Storage infrastructure

---

# Phase 4 – Monitoring

Deploy monitoring systems for:

- Water flow
- Water quality
- Temperature
- Pressure
- Pump health
- Energy usage
- Heat recovery
- Equipment status

---

# Phase 5 – AI Optimization

Enable software to support:

- Predictive maintenance
- Cooling optimization
- Pump scheduling
- Heat routing
- Water balancing
- Capacity forecasting

---

# Phase 6 – Operations

Routine activities include:

- Equipment inspections
- Water quality verification
- Sensor calibration
- Pump maintenance
- Heat exchanger maintenance
- Performance reporting

---

# Scalability

The architecture is intended to support modular expansion through additional cooling loops, equipment, and monitoring systems while maintaining a consistent operational framework.

---

# Project Status

This document describes a conceptual deployment approach for engineering discussion and future development.
